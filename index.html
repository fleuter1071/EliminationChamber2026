The Unforgiving Structure

The Elimination Chamber is a massive, circular steel cage that encloses the entire ring and ringside area. It is designed to punish and intimidate.

The Specs

  • Weight: 10 Tons of solid steel
  • Chain: 2 Miles of chain-link fencing
  • Diameter: 36 feet across
  • Pods: 4 inner enclosures made of reinforced plexiglass

The Rules

  • Two superstars start the match in the ring.
  • The remaining four are locked inside the corner pods.
  • At regular timed intervals, a pod opens at random, releasing another wrestler.
  • Eliminations occur only via pinfall or submission inside the ring.
  • The last person standing is the winner.

Origins & Brutal History

Before 2002, WWE fans only knew the traditional Steel Cage and Hell in a Cell. The Elimination Chamber changed the landscape of sports entertainment forever.

How It Came About

Introduced on-screen by Eric Bischoff in November 2002, the concept was the brainchild of Triple H. The goal was to combine the countdown timer of the Royal Rumble, the survival aspect of Survivor Series, and the enclosed cage brutality of WarGames.

The Inaugural Match

The very first match took place at Survivor Series 2002 at Madison Square Garden. Shawn Michaels famously survived the brutal debut, overcoming Triple H, Chris Jericho, Kane, Booker T, and Rob Van Dam to win the World Heavyweight Championship.

Evolution of the Chamber

Originally, the floor outside the ring was made of solid steel grating—a "death trap." In 2017, WWE updated the structure to a slightly more square-ish design with padded floors outside the ropes to better fit modern arenas and improve safety.
